<title>Conceptualizing organizational innovation: The case of the Brazilian software industry</title>


This paper presents and tests a conceptual model that explains the innovation practices and innovation outcomes in Brazilian software firms. <results>In terms of internal and external innovation capabilities, the ability to manage the relationship between people and their organizational contexts plays an important role in organizational innovation.
<results> 
	<result> 
		Based on these results, we propose that the disparities between software firms in building organizational innovation can be attributed, in part, to differences in their internal capabilities.
		<TypeOfResult>Qualitative</TypeOfResult>
	</result>
</results> The paper aims at encouraging and supporting managers to develop internal capabilities as a means of effectively leveraging the internally and externally distributed knowledge essential to innovation development.
